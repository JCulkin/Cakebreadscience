5.1–5.22 Density, Pressure and Gas Laws
Key Concepts: Density relates mass and volume. Pressure is force per area and also depends on depth in fluids. Gas pressure depends on temperature and volume.

Section A: Density and Pressure

1. A block has mass 2.4 kg and volume 0.003 m³. Calculate its density. [2]
2. A force of 200 N acts on an area of 0.5 m². Calculate pressure. [2]

Section B: Pressure in Fluids

3. Explain why pressure in a liquid acts equally in all directions. [2]
4. Calculate pressure at 3 m depth in water (ρ = 1000 kg/m³, g = 9.8 N/kg). [3]

Section C: Gas Laws and Temperature

5. Describe the motion of gas molecules and explain why there is an absolute zero temperature. [3]
6. Convert 25°C to Kelvin and 300 K to °C. [2]
7. A gas has pressure 100 kPa at 300 K. What is the pressure at 360 K (constant volume)? [2]
8. A gas has volume 2.0 dm³ at 100 kPa. What volume at 200 kPa (constant temperature)? [2]
Total marks: 18

Mark Scheme

1. ρ = m/V = 2.4/0.003 = 800 kg/m³ [2]
2. p = F/A = 200/0.5 = 400 Pa [2]
3. Fluid pressure acts in all directions because particles move randomly and collisions are equal [2]
4. p = hρg = 3 × 1000 × 9.8 = 29,400 Pa [3]
5. Gas molecules move randomly; pressure from collisions; absolute zero is when average kinetic energy is minimum [3]
6. 25°C = 298 K; 300 K = 27°C [2]
7. p2 = p1 T2/T1 = 100 × 360/300 = 120 kPa [2]
8. p1V1 = p2V2, V2 = (100×2.0)/200 = 1.0 dm³ [2]
