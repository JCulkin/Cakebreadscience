Light and Sound

IGCSE Edexcel Physics
3.14–3.23 Light, Refraction and Sound
Key Concepts: Light travels in straight lines. Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Refraction occurs when light enters different medium. Refractive index n = c/v. Lenses converge or diverge light rays.

Section A: Reflection and Refraction

1. State the laws of reflection. [2]
2. Light travels from air into glass. Use $n = \sin i / \sin r$ to calculate $r$ when $i=30°$ and $n=1.5$. [2]

Section B: Total Internal Reflection

3. Define critical angle and describe how total internal reflection occurs. [3]
4. Calculate the critical angle for glass with $n = 1.5$ using $n = 1/\sin c$. [2]

Section C: Applications

5. Explain how optical fibres use total internal reflection. [2]

Section D: Sound Waves

6. State whether sound is longitudinal or transverse and explain how sound can be reflected and refracted. [3]

Mark Scheme

1. Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal are coplanar [1]; Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection [1]
2. sin r = sin 30° / 1.5 = 0.333; r ≈ 19.5° [2]
3. Critical angle: angle of incidence giving 90° refraction; TIR occurs at angles greater than critical when travelling from denser to less dense medium [3]

5. Light repeatedly reflects inside fibre with no loss at boundary [2]
