8.1–8.10 Universe, Orbits and Stellar Evolution
Key Concepts: The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars. Orbits are caused by gravity. Stars change over time depending on mass.

Section A: Motion in the Universe

1. State what a galaxy is and where our solar system is located. [2]
2. Explain why gravitational field strength varies on different planets and on the Moon. [3]

Section B: Orbits

3. Explain how gravity causes moons, planets and satellites to orbit. [3]
4. Calculate orbital speed for a satellite with radius $r = 7.0 \times 10^6\,\text{m}$ and period $T = 6000\,\text{s}$. [2]

Section C: Stellar Evolution

5. Explain how star colour relates to temperature and classify red and blue stars. [2]
6. Describe the life cycle of a Sun-like star. [4]
7. Describe the evolution of a star more massive than the Sun. [4]
