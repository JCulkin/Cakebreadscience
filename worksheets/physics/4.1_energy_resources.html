Energy Transfers and Power

IGCSE Edexcel Physics
4.1–4.17 Energy Transfers, Efficiency, Work and Power
Key Concepts: Energy is transferred between stores. Use conservation of energy and efficiency. Thermal transfer occurs by conduction, convection and radiation.

Section A: Energy Stores and Transfers

1. Name the energy store for each scenario and the main transfer pathway. [4]
(a) A stretched spring released to move a toy
(b) A kettle heating water
2. Calculate GPE for a 3 kg object lifted 4 m (g = 9.8 N/kg). [2]

Section B: Efficiency and Sankey Diagrams

3. A motor transfers 2000 J of energy and 600 J is useful. Calculate efficiency. [2]
4. Describe what a Sankey diagram shows. [2]

Section C: Thermal Energy Transfer

5. Describe conduction, convection and radiation with one example each. [6]
6. Explain how to reduce unwanted energy transfer in a house. [3]

Section D: Work and Power

7. A 50 N force moves a box 3 m. Calculate the work done. [2]
8. A 10 kg object moves at 4 m/s. Calculate its kinetic energy. [2]
9. A motor does 600 J of work in 20 s. Calculate the power. [2]
Total marks: 25

Mark Scheme

1. Elastic → kinetic (mechanically); electrical → thermal (heating) [4]
2. GPE = 3 × 9.8 × 4 = 117.6 J [2]
3. Efficiency = 600/2000 × 100 = 30% [2]
4. Shows energy transfers with arrow widths proportional to energy [2]
5. Conduction through solids; convection in fluids; radiation as IR (any six points) [6]
6. Insulation, double glazing, draught excluders, reflective surfaces (any three) [3]
7. W = Fd = 50 × 3 = 150 J [2]
8. KE = 1/2 mv² = 0.5 × 10 × 16 = 80 J [2]
9. P = W/t = 600/20 = 30 W [2]
